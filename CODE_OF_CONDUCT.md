# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

Poincaré computing is foundational work bridging mathematics, physics, computer science, and epistemology. We are committed to providing a welcoming, respectful, and rigorous environment for all contributors regardless of:

- Age
- Body size
- Disability
- Ethnicity
- Gender identity and expression
- Level of experience
- Nationality
- Personal appearance
- Race
- Religion
- Sexual identity and orientation
- Socioeconomic status
- Technical background

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- **Respectful Communication**: Using welcoming and inclusive language
- **Constructive Feedback**: Providing helpful, actionable criticism
- **Collaborative Spirit**: Working together toward common goals
- **Intellectual Rigor**: Maintaining high standards of correctness and clarity
- **Patient Teaching**: Helping others understand complex concepts
- **Graceful Disagreement**: Disagreeing respectfully on technical matters
- **Acknowledgment**: Crediting others' ideas and contributions
- **Openness**: Accepting feedback and different viewpoints

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- **Harassment**: Trolling, insulting comments, personal or political attacks
- **Discrimination**: Exclusionary language or behavior based on protected characteristics
- **Privacy Violation**: Publishing others' private information without permission
- **Plagiarism**: Claiming others' work as your own
- **Academic Dishonesty**: Falsifying results, cherry-picking data, misleading citations
- **Bad Faith**: Intentionally wasting others' time, derailing discussions
- **Unprofessional Conduct**: Any conduct reasonably considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project spaces (GitHub repositories, issues, discussions, PRs)
- Official communication channels
- Project events and meetings
- Representing the project in public spaces

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. **For immediate safety concerns**: Contact local authorities
2. **For Code of Conduct violations**: Email [to be determined]
3. **For general concerns**: Use GitHub's reporting features

All reports will be:

- Reviewed promptly
- Handled confidentially
- Investigated fairly
- Responded to appropriately

### Consequences

Violations may result in:

1. **Warning**: Private written warning explaining violation
2. **Temporary Ban**: Temporary suspension from project spaces
3. **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from project community

The response depends on:

- Severity of violation
- Pattern of behavior
- Impact on community
- Willingness to correct behavior
